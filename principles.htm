Master Irv's Personal Philosophy

We strive to improve the quality of mankind through our teachings and philosophies and reach for goals of improvement in mind and body.

Karate and Kickboxing Philosophy

KNOWLEDGE - Knowledge of the forms and techniques of our art and their application regarding self defense.
 
SKILL - At each belt level you learn new Karate forms and Kickboxing techniques.  At each successive level you are expected to perform previously learned forms and techniques with greater skill than before.  All students are evaluated on the basis of their individual progress.  You are not expected to perform better in comparison to another student, only to be better than you were before.

ATTITUDE - Attitude is one of the most important parts of your training.  Without the proper emphasis on attitudinal development, Karate and Kickboxing are little more than organized brawling.

RESPECT - Gain respect for yourself and learn to treat others with a sense of regard, value and worthiness.
 
COURTESY - To behave kindly, considerately, and positively toward other people.
 
SELF DISCIPLINE - To overcome the opponent within you, who would lead you down the paths of laziness and self-aggrandizement, in an attempt to defeat you.

CONFIDENT HUMILITY - When confidence in your knowledge and skill is firm within you and you have no need to prove yourself to anyone, short of an actual self-defense situation in which your life or health, or that of a loved one is in jeopardy.



SCHOOL PROTOCOL

Everyone is required to bow upon entering or exiting the Dojo (training area).  All classes begin and end with a bow, as a sign of respect.  Students entering class late or dismissed early are also required to bow.  All black belts are referred to as Sensei (meaning Teacher) and must be bowed to upon greeting or departure. There are no exceptions to this protocol.
