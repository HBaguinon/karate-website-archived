Our Instructors

The Co-Owner and Head Instructor: KickBoxing 
Master Irv Althouse

Karate Master Irv Althouse holds a 7th Degree Black Belt in Kenpo Karate. With more than 25 years in the martial arts, Master Althouse is devoted to quality instruction and it shows in his students. Master Althouse has trained under Grand Master Alan K. Kates, Sensei Scott Lechi, Master Jerry Brennan, Master Christie Thomas, and Sensei Duke Redman.

Master Althouse believes in teaching all his students complete body and mind discipline, motivation, self-control, self-esteem and a sense of complete self-confidence. At Althouse Karate & Kickboxing Academy, Master Althouse and his team of highly skilled instructors strive to improve the quality of mankind through their teachings and philosophies, reaching for goals of improvement in mind and body.

Master Althouse was a longtime board member of the Worldwide Alliance of Martial Artists, a representative of the WPKA, and the owner of the United States Kickboxing Alliance. He is a licensed promoter with the PA State Athletic Commission and has promoted Kickboxing and Muay Thai events for the past 12 years.

Master Irv Althouse's list of accomplishments
November 10, 1997: Inducted into Eastern Alliance of Martial Arts Hall of Fame as Master Breaker.
August 15, 1998: Inducted into World Alliance of Martial Arts as Martial Artist of the year.
September 25, 1999: Master Althouse set a new world record for concrete block breaking (70 concrete blocks standing 16 feet high and weighing 1,650 pounds).
January 8, 2000: Inducted into the American Federation of Martial Arts Hall of Fame as Martial Artist of the year.
April 24-28, 2003: Head Coach for the USA Kickboxing team at the World Championships on Kos Island, Greece.
March 18-20, 2004: USA Head Coach - USA vs. South Africa. USA representative for the WPKA.

Co-Owner and Instructor
Louann Stewart

Louann holds a brown belt in Full Contact Kickboxing with nine years experience, and has fought in several amateur U.S.K.A. sanctioned kickboxing matches. She also trains in Chinese Kenpo Karate, and has trained in the martial art of Tai Chi Chu'an.

Master Tom Althouse - Instructor
Master Tom Althouse is a 5th degree black belt in Chinese Kenpo, and is one of the karate instructors at Althouse Karate Academy. He has been training in the martial arts for over 25 years. He holds many grand champions in karate competitions. He has been training with master Irv for the past 20 years, and first trained under Bart Ganster in Ishinru Karate. He holds a blue belt in full contact kickboxing, and has had 2 full contact fights in the ring. He is the corner trainer for the full contact kickboxers. He has also had some training in grappling and Japanese Kenpo.  Master Tom specializes in sparing for competition and self defense. Master Tom Althouse strives on teaching all his students to be the best all round martial artists they can be.




Master Christie Thomas - Instructor
After 40 years in the martial arts, Master Christie has achieved ranking in Chinese Kempo, Shotokan, Okinawan Kempo, Sa Gi Do, and Ko Sho Ryu. She continues to further her martial arts training while studying with Grandmaster Wei Brown in Shodo and Kanji. Master Christie has been recognized by the PA Hall of Fame, and PA Karate Hall of Fame with special awards for her martial arts achievements. She specializes in teaching young children.

Sensei Kevin Kauffman - Assistant Fight Trainer
Kevin has been involved in the martial arts for more than 17 years. He holds a 3rd degree black belt in Chinese Kempo. Kevin also holds a black belt in kickboxing.

In 2003 Kevin travelled to Greece for the world kickboxing championships. While in Greece Kevin fought the 3 time world champion and came home with a bronze medal.
Kevin travelled to Canada for Nationals twice. He was 1st in kickboxing twice and 1st and 2nd in karate point-sparring.

His USA Fight Record is 5-1 Kickboxing and 1-0





Sensei Harold Baguinon - Instructor
Harold has been a martial artist for over 20 years. He holds a 2nd degree black belt, earned here at Althouse Karate & Kickboxing Academy. He also holds a brown belt in Tae Kwon Do, a green belt in Japanese Shotokan Karate, and a Yellow Belt in Judo/Jujutsu. His martial arts background also includes Filipino Escrima, boxing, kickboxing, Wing Chun Kung Fu, American Kenpo, and he has dabbled in Bajiquan, Kendo, and Iaido.

In 2003, Harold attended the University of Pittsburgh, where he studied Shotokan, Tae Kwon Do, Judo, and Escrima. There, he trained with several martial arts clubs, and in 2005, started his own, known as Martial Arts Collaboration. At MAC, martial artists from all styles were encouraged to share their experiences and knowledge, teach each other new philosophies, and test one another in a sparring session.

In 2010, Harold moved to Japan, where he continued to study Japanese martial arts with the Japanese Karate Association, and Judo with his own students. Having returned to the US, we are happy to have Harold back to share his many experiences.
